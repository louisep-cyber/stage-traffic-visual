Reality Baseline · 1 of 4

What is The Stage?

The Stage is actually a bundle of things. These things probably have different futures.
Ad Product

Rich-media, full-screen advertising experience sold to brand advertisers. Includes both custom builds and templated (GA) executions.

Platform

An embedded webview within Spotify's mobile apps. Delivers authenticated web experiences that leverage native mobile features — playback, sharing, playlist adds, data-driven stories.

Stage Studio & Improv

Self-serve CMS used by creative production vendors to build and manage Stage experiences. Improv supports the templated product — currently used by one internal team.

Consumer Experience

The Stage powers key Spotify consumer products including Wrapped, Playlist Cover Art, and Library Import — experiences used by tens of millions of users.

  • We don't have an accurate revenue picture
  • We don't report on value accurately

Breaks Core Spotify Consumer UX

  • Takes users out of their consumption context
  • Hides play controls
  • Doesn't allow seamless navigation in and out

Addiction to Driver Media

  • No organic activity associated with the Stage
  • Pure driver media play limits value, reach, and quality

Lack of Product Novelty

  • "We have nothing new to pitch"
  • Stagnant product limits sales excitement and client renewal
Sponsorship Strategy

Where does The Stage fit?

There is a place for custom brand activations on Spotify — but today's Stage is a very imperfect meeting of that need. We should do better.
There IS a space in our sponsorship offering

A very-custom integration with Spotify has a home in our portfolio — but only if priced appropriately and if it delivers true listener value.

Today's Stage is the closest thing we have — but it's imperfect

As covered: measurement gaps, UX issues, driver dependency, and no new product to pitch. It approximates the vision, but falls short.

We believe we DO need an offering in this space

The question is whether we evolve The Stage or build toward something more strategically aligned. We recommend doing both in parallel.

Recommendation

Our proposal for 2026

The Stage as it exists today is here for 2026 — but not 2027. We use the rest of 2026 to build the bridge to something better.
1

Enter maintenance mode on the Stage ad product for 2026

No new investment in the ad product itself. Hold the line.

2

Pause development on Studio and Improv entirely

Free up capacity. No new feature development on the CMS tooling.

3

Build a tech strategy and staffing plan for Stage Platform ownership

Security review. Determine resourcing: if we keep the platform, we need 1 SR PM + additional engineers to support it AND deliver sponsorship work.

4

Spike: what would it take to normalise the Stage as a strategic ad product?

Logging & measurement, pricing thresholds, spin down Stage GA, organic distribution, fix consumer UX, re-evaluate driver package.

5

Continue building alternative sponsorship value through new products

Now: Compound Sponsored Playlists  ·  Next: Beefed-up takeover via app open  ·  Next next: Sponsored Premium Day, Sound Capsule, Premium Features
